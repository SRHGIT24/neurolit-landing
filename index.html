How NeuroLit works

A five-stage pipeline from question to grounded answer — no retrieval step skipped.

01
Biomedical query encoding
Your question is encoded by a biomedical-specific encoder trained on millions of scientific abstracts — not a generic language model.
02
Section-aware retrieval
42,831 chunks — split by paper section: methods, results, limitations, discussion — are searched by semantic similarity. Top 20 candidates returned.
03
Precision reranking
A cross-encoder scores each question-chunk pair jointly. More precise than embedding similarity alone. Top 5 selected for generation.
04
Grounded generation
The generation model synthesises only from retrieved passages. It cannot cite papers not in the retrieved context. Fabrication is architecturally prevented.
05
Evidence calibration
Output distinguishes in vitro, animal, and human evidence. Flags conflicting findings between papers. Reports gaps explicitly.

Privacy Policy

What we collect

NeuroLit does not collect personal data. When you submit a research query, the question text is sent to our API to generate a response. We do not store queries, link them to individuals, or use them for any purpose beyond generating your answer.

Cookies

NeuroLit does not use tracking cookies or analytics cookies. No third-party advertising or tracking scripts are present on this site.

Third-party services

Query processing involves third-party AI infrastructure. Query text may pass through these services solely to generate your answer and is not retained or used for training.

Research disclaimer

NeuroLit synthesises publicly available scientific literature. It is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations. Always consult qualified clinicians and primary literature.
